likes: drawing, Japan, Bob Dylan, e-biking, coding, good black coffee, the planet...

 

The 'ei' logo is inspired by the seals used in Japan, China and SE Asia to 'sign' documents and by artists to sign their work

I write apps for my own use but some are freely available for anyone to use, but... no guarantees - they are generally harmless but you use them at your own risk.
All the apps are PWAs - progressive web apps - which you access like any web page but which can install on your phone and work without a network connection.
Using the apps is mainly a matter of tapping and swiping. For example, swipe left to cancel a dialog, swipe right to go back. All data is held on your device and most of the apps save monthly backups making it easy to transfer to a new phone.

  • Thomas Bodley, a 17th-century diplomat and scholar founded Oxford's Bodleian library, a world-famous repository of information.

    This app is an information repository for your phone. Bodley allows you to add notes about whatever you like, organised in lists or lists of lists.

  • Isambard Kingdom Brunel had to design his bridges and ships without the benefit of computer-aided design.

    This app is a very basic 2D CAD app you can use on your tablet or Chromebook. Brunel lets you produce monochrome scale drawings in sizes up to A4.

  • Victor Pasmore was one of Britain's leading pioneer abstract artists. His work soon evolved from the early figurative paintings to the colourful, two-dimensional and graphical abstract images for which he is best known.

    This app is a 2D drawing app - similar in many ways to the Brunel app without the scaling and dimensions but with colour and patterns, background iamges and freehand sketching.

  • Samuel Pepys was, amongst other things, Britain's most famous diarist, chronicling the plague and the Great Fire of London in the sixteen-hundreds.

    This app lets you record significant events and activities and label them, making them easy to find by date, label or content.

  • Dickens' Scrooge, a character from A Christmas Carol is famously, or notoriously, careful with money.

    This app is for the Scrooge in all of us and lets you keep track of your bank accounts, savings, investments, etc. Boring but useful.

  • Warhammer's Slaanesh may not necessarily be a nice guy to know, but his Keeper of Secrets daemons are excellent at keeping mum.

    This app is to keep lists of anything you prefer not to share: bank PINs and logins, website credentials, safe combinations,... whatever. They are held on your phone in encrypted form and to open the app you enter a four-digit PIN

  • James Watt is another outstanding British engineer from back in the day. He gave his name to one of the best known SI units used, among other things, to measure heat loss.

    This app allows a relatively quick and easy estimation of the fabric heat losses from a building as an aid at the design stage or to assess possible retrofit measures. You define building elements (walls, roof, openings, etc) by area and construction using a menu of predefined materials or specifying your own.
