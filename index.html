I wrote all these apps for my own use but they are freely available for anyone to use with no guarantees. They are generally harmless but you use them at your own risk.
All the apps are PWAs - progressive web apps - which you access like any web page but which can install on your phone and work without a network connection.
Using the apps is mainly a matter of tapping and swiping. For example, swipe left to cancel a dialog, swipe right to go back.

  • Thomas Bodley, a 17th-century diplomat and scholar founded Oxford's Bodleian library, a world-famous repository of information.

    This app is an information repository for your phone. Bodley allows you to add notes about whatever you like, organised in lists or lists of lists.

  • Isambard Kingdom Brunel had to design his brdges and ships without the benefit of computer-aided design.

    This app is a very basic 2D CAD app you can use on your tablet or Chromebook. Brunel allows you to add notes about whatever you like, organised in lists or lists of lists.
